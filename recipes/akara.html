Fluffy Homemage Akara

Description

Akara is a traditional Nigeria dish made of blended beans and deep fried.

Ingredients

Here is the list of ingredients needed to make Akara:

  • 2 cups of black-eyed peas (or brown beans) - peeled and washed
  • 1 small onion - chopped
  • 1-2 Scotch bonnet peppers (or habanero) - to tased, chopped
  • Salt - to taste
  • Water - just a little, to blend (about a quarter to half cup)
  • Vegetable oil - for deep fying (palm oil can be added for flavor, but it's optional)
  • Seasoning cubes
  • Crayfish (ground) - for a richer taste
  • Green onion or spring onions - chopped
  • Bell pepper - for color and mild seetness
  • Ginger or garlic - for added aroma (used sparingly)

Steps

Here are the steps for making Akara:
  1. Peel the beans:
    • Soak the beans in water for 10-15 minutes.
    • Rub the soaked beans between your palms or pulse briefly in a blender to help loosen the skins.
    • Rinse and drain several times to remove the skins and leave only the white inner beans.
  2. Blend the beans:
    • Add the peeled beans, chopped onions, and peppers to a blender.
    • Add just a little water (start with ¼ cup) — the batter should be thick, not watery.
    • Blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. Season the Batter:
    • Pour the blended batter into a bowl.
    • Add salt to taste and any optional ingredients (e.g., ground crayfish, seasoning cubes).
    • Beat the batter with a spoon or whisk for a few minutes to incorporate air — this helps make the akara fluffy.
  4. Heat the Oil:
    • Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or pot over medium heat.
    • The oil should be hot but not smoking — test with a tiny drop of batter; it should sizzle and float.
  5. Fry the Akara:
    • Scoop the batter with a spoon or your hand and carefully drop small mounds into the oil.
    • Do not overcrowd the pan — fry in batches.
    • Fry until golden brown on all sides (about 2–3 minutes per side).
    • Remove and drain on paper towels.
  6. Serve:
    • Serve hot with pap (ogi), custard, bread, or as a snack on its own.
